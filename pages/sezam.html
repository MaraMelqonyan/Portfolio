Sezam Shop

OVERVIEW

Sezam's mission is to make your life better, and the shopping process is almost magical, sacred..

The goal of this particular project was to identify any usability issues for the store and add onboarding, customize the UX solution.

ROLE

UX/UI Designer

Interaction, Visual design, Testing

Background

A group of young people once discovered that purchases in Russia are of the same type and prices are quite high. Marketplaces in Russia hardly change and do not offer new solutions. They, under the leadership of Nikita Maksimov and Edil Pchelkin, looked at the best ideas for improving the shopping process - in China, the USA, and Europe. They added their ideas - and systematically applied them in sezam.shop

Understanding the problem

I conducted a brainstorming workshop with the team to understand what was the problem that prevents users from understanding the shop. It was revealed that:
  • Users didn't understand how the project works
  • It was difficult for users to understand where to click after entering the store
  • It was difficult for users to understand how the shopping system works

What is onboarding for?

The goal was to understand what onboarding was used for. We marked a couple of points:
  • Increase the conversion of a lead into sales
  • Working off all objections
  • Involvement in the ideology of the project
  • In less time initiate an ambassador
  • Help navigate the project
  • Help find what you want
  • Evoke emotions
  • Demonstration of value
  • Conversion to registration

Elements to be used in onboarding

To create onboarding, I discussed with the team with what elements they wanted to demonstrate onboarding. They noted the following:
  • Popup or large block. In a header on almost the entire screen
  • Welcome and value engagement headline
  • CTA on the start pop-up or start block with a call to indicate interests
  • Welcom video with value engagement
  • Illustrations with value engagement
  • Price comparison service
